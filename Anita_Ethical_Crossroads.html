Anita's Ethical Crossroads

You are Anita Dabolkar, Assistant Superintendent at the India Post (IP), a public institution known for strict rules and deep public trust.

During a field inspection, you uncover irregularities in financial records managed by Meena Thirchandani — a respected, long-serving Branch Head celebrated for her performance.

What will you do as events unfold? Your choices will reveal how different ethical perspectives guide real leadership decisions.

Inspection – Discovery of Misappropriation

You find several customer receipts that are not accounted properly (not entered in official records, which amounts to financial misappropriation). Meena seems tense and has no proper justification.

What should Anita do?

A. Recommend Immediate Suspension
B. Issue a Confidential Warning and Allow Restitution
C. Escalate to Higher Authority Without Immediate Action

You chose: Recommend Immediate Suspension

You recommend immediate suspension and formal disciplinary action.

The inquiry confirms misappropriation. Meena is removed, new controls are introduced, and public trust in India Post is strengthened.
However, local leaders protest and staff morale in the region drops.

This choice followed a Rules-First approach
You believed some rules must never be broken, no matter who is involved or what the short-term cost is.

You chose: Issue a Confidential Warning and Allow Restitution

You give Meena a strong confidential warning and 15 days to return the money quietly.

Money is returned, no scandal occurs, peace is maintained, and targets are met. Superiors appreciate your practical handling.
Eighteen months later, another branch head repeats the same offence, saying “it was allowed once”. An audit exposes multiple cases. You are held accountable for setting a bad precedent.

This choice followed an Outcomes-First approach
You focused on reducing immediate harm, even if it meant bending procedure this one time.

You chose: Escalate to Higher Authority Without Immediate Action

You immediately inform the Divisional Superintendent, present facts calmly, and propose a fair, transparent process that allows Meena to explain and make restitution under supervision.

A transparent committee is formed. Meena returns the full amount and accepts a pay cut + transfer. New ethics training is rolled out nationwide. Staff see the process as fair and caring. Your handling is praised as a model of mature leadership.

This choice followed a Wisdom-and-Balance approach
You looked for an honest middle path that respected rules, protected people, and strengthened the organisation in the long run.
